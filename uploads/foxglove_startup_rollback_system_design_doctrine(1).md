# Foxglove Startup & Rollback Reboot Sequence
# System Design Doctrine & Visual Bible
## Reality Reconstruction Through Text
### Presented through a Liminal Corporate Vaporwave System

**Status:** Canon design doctrine  
**Primary use:** Foxglove startup, rollback initiation, total-system reboot, continuity reconstruction, and portal handoff  
**Core principle:** Rollback is the machine reconstructing reality through text. The retro-liminal Macintosh, Windows 95, Japanese-commercial, CRT, and vaporwave art direction is the presentation language for that reconstruction. This visual language may appear lightly during ordinary startup, but its full artistic and narrative **heyday** belongs to rollback, where the entire Foxglove system is deliberately dismantled and reality is rebuilt from institutional data.

---

## 0. Document purpose

This document defines the **complete design doctrine, art direction, motion language, structural logic, narrative role, lifecycle, and implementation rules** for the **Foxglove startup and rollback reboot sequence**.

It is intended to function as a **source-of-truth bible** for:

- Claude Design
- Figma design work
- motion design
- implementation planning
- engineering handoff
- art direction reviews
- future asset creation
- startup-sequence iteration
- rollback-sequence iteration
- recurrence and continuity storytelling
- audiovisual timing, excluding any dependency on music
- long-term expansion of the simulation-reset presentation layer

This is not a loose inspiration note.

It is a **design doctrine**: a statement of **what the Foxglove startup and rollback reboot are, what they are not, how they should feel, how they should behave, where the style should be restrained, where it should reach its fullest expression, and how all future work should be judged**.

The defining implementation intention is now:

> Initialising rollback reboots the entire Foxglove system. The machine does not merely reload an interface; it reconstructs reality through text. Employee records, corporate language, system metadata, filenames, identifiers, performance data, remembered fragments, and authorised persistence become the raw visual material from which the next simulation is assembled.

The liminal corporate vaporwave style does not replace this concept. It gives that process its final visual identity: Macintosh Plus restraint, Windows 95 procedural familiarity, Japanese commercial optimism, CRT softness, pastel kitsch futurism, and empty digital liminality wrapped around a deeper act of machine-led reality reconstruction.

The ordinary startup may introduce the vocabulary.  
The rollback sequence owns the complete grammar.

# 1. Core definition

## 1.1 One-sentence definition

The Foxglove rollback sequence is **the machine reconstructing reality through text**, presented as a **liminal corporate vaporwave boot ritual** that blends Macintosh Plus restraint, Japanese commercial graphics, Windows 95 desktop culture, tasteful kitsch retro aesthetics, ASCII reconstruction, CRT display language, and uncanny corporate memory corruption.

---

## 1.2 Expanded definition

The startup and rollback sequence should feel like:

- an alternate-history enterprise operating system
- a forgotten workplace boot environment from the late 1980s or 1990s
- a polished but eerie onboarding ritual
- a nostalgic desktop dream-space
- a corporate system reconstructing identity from damaged memory
- a beautiful, softly commercial, slightly empty digital space
- an old training-video optimism beginning to decay
- a procedural continuity engine restarting reality from administrative records
- a total institutional reboot disguised as routine system maintenance
- a company operating system calmly rebuilding the employee after a simulation rollback

It should not feel like a generic intro cinematic.  
It should not feel like a music visualiser.  
It should not feel like a cyberpunk hacker interface.  
It should feel like **Foxglove itself waking up, shutting down, recovering, and assembling the employee’s reality**.

---

## 1.3 The primary concept: reality reconstructed through text

This is the governing idea for the rollback sequence.

The machine does not reconstruct reality by showing a conventional loading bar over a finished world. It reconstructs the world visibly, using language and data as matter.

During rollback:

- environments become text before they disappear
- employee portraits dissolve into identifiers and records
- documents become filenames, metadata, and repeated phrases
- offices collapse into grids, labels, and architectural strings
- system values become visual terrain
- persistent state remains legible while disposable state loses coherence
- memories appear as partial photographic forms inside text fields
- corporate language becomes both the blueprint and the material of the next simulation
- the new world emerges from the same textual substrate that consumed the old one

The player should feel that reality in Foxglove is not rendered first and described second.

It is **described first and rendered from that description**.

### Canon statement

> Foxglove reality is assembled from institutional language. Rollback exposes that construction process.

### What the retro art direction contributes

The new art direction determines how that reconstruction is experienced:

- **Macintosh Plus influence** gives it restraint, elegance, and early-computer tactility.
- **Windows 95 influence** gives it procedural familiarity and corporate desktop logic.
- **Japanese commercial graphics** give it polish, optimism, and poised visual composition.
- **Vaporwave** gives it nostalgia, melancholy, temporal displacement, and synthetic beauty.
- **Liminal space design** gives it emptiness, suspension, and the feeling of being between realities.
- **CRT and ASCII treatment** make the machine’s reconstruction process physically visible.

The new art style is therefore not an adjacent layer added on top of rollback.

It is the **surface language through which reality reconstruction becomes legible**.

---

## 1.4 Ordinary startup versus rollback reboot

The same visual language may appear in both ordinary startup and rollback, but the two states must not be visually or narratively equivalent.

### Ordinary startup

Ordinary startup should feel like:

- a controlled boot
- a normal return to the employee environment
- a familiar ritual
- a restrained expression of the style
- a short identity and continuity check
- a stable system resuming a known state

It may include:

- small terminal messages
- limited CRT bloom
- brief ASCII reconstruction
- one or two floating retro panels
- a short Foxglove mark formation
- a clean portal handoff

Ordinary startup is the **introduction and reminder** of the visual language.

### Rollback reboot

Rollback should feel like:

- the system being intentionally dismantled
- the current simulation state being terminated
- continuity being severed and rebuilt
- institutional memory being audited, compressed, filtered, and selectively retained
- employee identity being reconstituted
- the entire Foxglove environment being restarted from a deeper systems layer
- a ceremonial but bureaucratically controlled apocalypse
- a rebirth framed as routine administrative procedure

Rollback is the **full narrative, emotional, and visual heyday** of the style because it is the only moment when the player watches the machine expose its world-building process directly: reality is reduced to text, classified, partially remembered, and reconstructed.

### Canon rule

> The rollback sequence must use the complete visual vocabulary with greater duration, stronger scene progression, more explicit reconstruction, deeper memory fragments, more dramatic system traversal, and a more meaningful final handoff than ordinary startup.

---

## 1.5 Rollback is a reboot of the whole system

Rollback is not merely:

- a save reset
- a prestige-button animation
- a decorative transition
- a fade to black
- a statistics summary
- a simple profile reset

Rollback is the moment where the game does not merely imply, but visually demonstrates, that:

- the active simulation is being ended
- the current employee environment is no longer trusted
- Foxglove’s continuity layer is taking control
- the entire software reality is being reinitialised
- persistent elements are being extracted
- non-persistent state is being discarded
- the player is being rebuilt into a new run
- physical and interface reality are generated from textual institutional records
- the system is preserving exactly what it is authorised to remember

This should be legible emotionally, even before the player fully understands it narratively.

# 2. Foundational doctrine

## 2.1 The startup is a ritual, not a splash screen

The startup is not merely a loading state.  
It is a **ritualised threshold** between the real player and the Foxglove simulation.

It performs several jobs simultaneously:

1. **Branding**  
   It introduces Foxglove’s aesthetic world.

2. **Narrative framing**  
   It establishes that the player is entering a controlled system.

3. **Atmosphere setting**  
   It creates tone before the interface appears.

4. **Simulation foreshadowing**  
   It hints that this world is procedural, unstable, and recursive.

5. **Identity reconstruction**  
   It suggests the player is not simply opening a menu; they are being assembled, assigned, and loaded into an employee environment.

6. **Continuity conditioning**  
   It teaches the player that startup, shutdown, and rollback are all system-mediated transitions.

7. **Rollback foreshadowing**  
   It introduces visual motifs that later become fully legible during a complete system reboot.

---

## 2.2 The rollback is a system event, not a reward animation

Rollback must feel larger than an ordinary prestige reward screen.

It should communicate that:

- the current world-state has reached a terminal condition
- Foxglove has accepted the rollback request
- the simulation is being taken offline
- employee memory is being processed
- authorised persistence is being separated from discarded state
- reality is being reconstructed under corporate control

The player should feel they have initiated something consequential.

### Rule

**Rollback must be ceremonial, systemic, and slightly frightening, while remaining calm and administratively composed.**

---

## 2.3 The sequence must begin as comfort, then drift into unease

The sequence should initially feel:

- nostalgic
- polished
- gentle
- retro
- safe
- aesthetically pleasing
- corporate but charming

Then, gradually, it should begin to feel:

- eerily empty
- slightly over-controlled
- liminal
- emotionally distant
- subtly recursive
- quietly corrupted
- aware of something it should not know

This tonal shift is one of the most important principles in the entire bible.

### Rule

**The sequence should seduce first, unsettle second.**

It should never begin at maximum hostility or maximum glitch intensity.  
Foxglove’s horror, uncanniness, and simulation residue are more effective when they **emerge from corporate-soft nostalgia**.

---

## 2.4 The startup introduces; rollback reveals

The ordinary startup may suggest:

- identity assembly
- continuity checks
- institutional memory
- controlled anomaly
- system layers beneath the portal

The rollback sequence should reveal the larger meaning of those motifs.

For example:

- A portrait briefly reconstructed at startup becomes a full employee-state disassembly and rebuild during rollback.
- A small “continuity check” label at startup becomes a complete continuity audit during rollback.
- A single floating retro dialog becomes an entire liminal operating environment.
- A short ASCII flower formation becomes a full Foxglove convergence ritual.
- A tiny anomaly becomes a visible conflict between remembered and authorised state.

### Canon rule

> Every major visual motif introduced in ordinary startup should have a larger, more consequential rollback form.

---

## 2.5 The sequence is not a parody collage

Although the sequence draws from vaporwave, retro operating systems, Japanese commercial graphics, and kitsch nostalgia, it must never feel like:

- a meme board
- a random aesthetic mashup
- a list of internet references
- ironic design for its own sake
- superficial retro clutter

Everything should feel **coherent, curated, and purposeful**.

### Rule

**The sequence must feel authored, not assembled.**

# 5. Narrative function

## 5.1 What the startup means in-world

In-world, the startup sequence should be interpreted as a **Foxglove system initialisation environment**.

It is the visual process by which the system:

- recognises the employee
- loads their simulation state
- reconstructs identity
- seeds department alignment
- prepares the employee environment
- filters and stabilises memory
- suppresses anomalies
- hands control to the main portal

---

## 5.2 What rollback means in-world

In-world, rollback should be interpreted as a **controlled continuity reset**.

It is the visual process by which the system:

- accepts rollback authorisation
- freezes the active employee environment
- records final simulation state
- audits the run
- calculates retained value
- extracts authorised persistence
- isolates prohibited or unstable memory
- terminates active systems
- collapses the current simulated workspace
- resets non-persistent state
- reboots the entire environment
- reconstructs the employee
- reopens the portal under a new continuity instance
- reconstructs the visible world from text, metadata, memory fragments, and authorised records

---

## 5.3 Hidden narrative reading

At a deeper narrative level, the sequence suggests:

- the world is assembled rather than naturally continuous
- the player has likely been here before
- previous cycles have left behind traces
- the system’s memory is incomplete
- corporate branding hides deeper instability
- identity is procedural and editable
- reality is not fully trustworthy
- rollback is not time travel in a conventional sense
- recurrence is an institutional process
- continuity is a managed resource
- memory survival is permission-based
- Foxglove may be preserving more than the player is told
- the employee may be reconstructed from records rather than carried forward intact

These ideas should be implied, not explained.

---

## 5.4 The rollback sequence as the style’s heyday

The rollback sequence is where the visual doctrine reaches maximum narrative legitimacy.

It is the ideal place for:

- the longest ASCII reconstruction shots
- the richest retro GUI environment
- the strongest Macintosh Plus and Windows 95 influence
- the clearest Japanese-commercial composition
- the deepest liminal-space sequences
- the most meaningful CRT degradation
- the most expansive pastel and chrome atmosphere
- the strongest continuity anomalies
- the clearest transition from beauty into unease
- the most complete Foxglove logo convergence
- the deepest sense that the whole world is being rebooted

### Canon rule

> The style should not be exhausted during normal startup. Ordinary startup should preserve anticipation for rollback.

# 6. Core design pillars

All work must obey these pillars.

## 6.1 Pillar 1 — Retro corporate authenticity

The sequence must feel rooted in real retro workplace computing culture.

It should feel like it comes from:

- training systems
- old enterprise software
- desktop OS startup logic
- presentation software
- onboarding environments
- office-tech optimism
- personnel systems
- administrative recovery tools
- continuity management software

Not from sci-fi warfare, neon dystopia, or hacker fiction.

---

## 6.2 Pillar 2 — Liminal emptiness

There must be space, pause, and absence.

The sequence should frequently feel like:

- the office exists before the employee arrives
- the software is running without a visible operator
- the environment is prepared for someone who is not there yet
- the system is waiting
- the prior simulation has already ended
- the new simulation has not yet begun
- the player is temporarily between identities

This emptiness is essential.

---

## 6.3 Pillar 3 — Text as matter

Text is not only informational. It is also visual material.

Words, codes, strings, labels, symbols and metadata should serve as:

- texture
- image structure
- mask material
- portrait substance
- environment scaffolding
- motion particles
- continuity residue
- retained memory fragments
- erasure material
- state reconstruction components

The system should feel literally built from language and data.

During rollback, this becomes explicit: text is not an overlay on top of reality. Text is the intermediate state between one reality and the next.

---

## 6.4 Pillar 4 — Soft beauty before corruption

Foxglove should not reveal all its instability immediately.  
The sequence should first be alluring and emotionally readable.

It may become strange, but it should not open as chaos.

---

## 6.5 Pillar 5 — Controlled anomaly

Any corruption, self-awareness, or wrongness should feel:

- brief
- curated
- systemically plausible
- meaningful
- not random noise

Foxglove is not a broken game.  
It is an over-controlled system whose seams occasionally show.

During rollback, anomalies may last longer than during ordinary startup, but they must still feel contained within a process.

---

## 6.6 Pillar 6 — Clean handoff into normality

The boot or reboot sequence must eventually stabilise into a more normal interface.

This contrast matters.  
If the startup, rollback, and main UI are equally surreal, the sequence loses meaning.

---

## 6.7 Pillar 7 — Lifecycle discipline

This visual style must have a lifecycle.

It should move through:

- **Onboarding:** lightly introduced during first startup
- **Prime:** fully expressed during first major rollback
- **Expansion:** gains new motifs as lucidity and recurrence deepen
- **Taper:** ordinary starts become faster and more familiar
- **Legacy:** late-game rollbacks reinterpret earlier motifs with new meaning

This prevents the art direction from becoming decorative wallpaper.

---

- nightclub energy

## 7.3 Not meme vaporwave

Avoid:

- ironic clutter
- random Greek statues

- over-the-top collage humour
- internet aesthetic-board energy

## 7.4 Not full horror

Avoid making the sequence feel like:

- a jumpscare sequence
- overt corruption from frame one

- grotesque imagery

## 7.5 Not a music video

Avoid:

- beat-synchronised edits
- lyrical pacing
- visualiser logic
- rhythm-first sequencing

- arbitrary power-up effects
- fantasy rebirth symbolism
- a purely celebratory reward reveal
- a short summary screen with no system consequences

Rollback must feel like a world-scale administrative process.

# 8. Reality reconstruction doctrine

## 8.1 Reality has three visible states

Rollback should move the world through three conceptually distinct states:

### State A — Rendered reality

The familiar Foxglove portal exists as an apparently stable workplace environment.

It contains:

- panels
- portraits
- documents
- counters
- departments
- calendars
- employee records
- navigation
- active systems

At this stage, the world appears complete.

### State B — Textual substrate

The rendered environment is stripped back into its machine-readable foundation.

Objects become:

- identifiers
- filenames
- metadata
- status labels
- repeating phrases
- ASCII fields
- procedural descriptions
- system records
- memory classifications
- continuity markers

This is the true visual heart of rollback.

### State C — Reconstructed reality

The textual substrate is reorganised into a new environment.

The machine:

- restores authorised persistence
- creates a new continuity instance
- rebuilds the employee model
- reapplies department identity
- reforms visual objects
- stabilises the portal
- hides the textual substrate beneath a normal interface again

The rollback arc is therefore:

> **Reality → Text → Reality**

---

## 8.2 Text must transform, not merely decorate

ASCII and text fields must not function as a cosmetic filter placed over finished imagery.

The animation should show genuine transformations such as:

- a portrait turning into employee-record strings
- an office wall unravelling into repeated filenames
- a document stack collapsing into metadata rows
- a GUI panel flattening into system labels
- a corridor rebuilding from alignment and coordinate text
- a foxglove flower emerging from repeated continuity strings
- a department colour field being generated from tagged text
- a human eye resolving from performance metrics and memory fragments

### Rule

If the underlying object could be removed and the text layer would merely look like decoration, the reconstruction is too shallow.

The text should carry the object’s form, luminance, structure, and meaning.

---

## 8.3 Reconstruction should operate at several scales

### Micro scale

Individual glyphs, symbols, numbers, and words act like pixels.

### Object scale

Portraits, flowers, windows, files, and office objects are formed from text.

### Environmental scale

Corridors, rooms, desktop fields, and horizons are built from text architecture.

### System scale

Entire states such as ACTIVE SIMULATION, ARCHIVE, CONTINUITY, and REINITIALISATION become visual worlds.

### Narrative scale

The player understands that the simulation itself is textual, procedural, and institutionally authored.

---

## 8.4 The machine should appear to understand reality administratively

Foxglove does not reconstruct the world through poetic natural language.

It understands reality through:

- records
- statuses
- permissions
- classifications
- role assignments
- performance values
- department tags
- timestamps
- employee IDs
- file structures
- compliance language
- system messages
- retained-state manifests

This is critical to the satire.

The machine’s ontology is corporate.

A person is represented as:

- profile
- grade
- department
- capability
- status
- history
- productivity
- authorised memory

An office is represented as:

- workspace
- access state
- queue
- schedule
- document system
- environment configuration

The rollback sequence should make that worldview visible.

---

## 8.5 Reconstruction should preserve ambiguity

The sequence should not explain every conversion literally.

Some transformations should remain emotionally suggestive:

- a face hidden in repeated words
- a corridor built from employee names
- a flower emerging from continuity strings
- an eye formed from performance metrics
- an office scene remembered as a broken product advertisement
- a figure appearing briefly inside a text field before being classified away

The player should understand the process without fully understanding every image.

# 10. Visual language bible

## 8.1 Global canvas logic

The sequence should usually operate within a **16:9 frame** and alternate between:

- black void
- pastel atmospheric field
- desktop-like empty space
- floating panels
- full-screen ASCII reconstruction
- centred graphic stage
- constrained card-like presentation
- liminal environmental compositions
- recursive systems chambers
- state-compression fields
- portal-like continuity tunnels

The frame should sometimes feel cinematic, and sometimes feel like a piece of system software that has become strangely beautiful.

---

## 8.2 Negative space doctrine

Negative space is essential.

### Use black space for:

- suspense
- elegance
- focus
- system silence
- emotional emptiness
- visual reset
- simulation death
- absence between continuity instances

### Use pastel empty space for:

- dreamlike suspension
- ad-like optimism
- waiting-room liminality
- nostalgic softness
- identity calibration
- corporate future-of-work fantasy

### Rule

**Never fill every frame with detail.**  
Foxglove needs emptiness to breathe.

---

## 8.3 Composition doctrine

Use a mixture of:

- centred compositions
- floating-object-in-void compositions
- full-bleed data fields
- asymmetrical Japanese-ad-inspired arrangements
- desktop window groupings
- portrait-stage compositions
- grid-aligned system layouts
- recursive tunnel compositions
- framed memory snapshots
- false product-demonstration layouts
- sterile corporate tableau

### Preferred composition traits

- deliberate breathing room
- graphic clarity
- poised asymmetry
- simple geometric anchors
- calm balance
- occasional architectural symmetry
- meaningful isolation
- controlled scale changes
- restrained information density

- cool grey-white
- CRT grey

- pale stone

### Retro hardware neutrals

---

## 9.3 Main vaporwave-corporate palette

Use in gradients, UI surfaces, glow layers, soft environmental fields and memory-space compositions.

- pale cyan
- washed sky blue
- pastel lavender
- soft lilac
- dusty pink
- light peach
- mint green
- desaturated teal
- chrome silver
- soft violet-grey

---

## 9.4 Accent palette

Reserved for emphasis, transitions and anomaly moments.

- hot magenta
- electric cyan
- vivid teal
- amber
- warning red
- acid lime
- cobalt blue
- salmon rose

Use sparingly.

---

## 9.5 Department colour integration

At some point in the sequence, department identity may influence the visual palette.

This colour introduction should feel like:

- a calibration pass
- a system assignment
- a heat-map tint
- a seeded identity layer
- a department profile being loaded into the employee model

It should not feel like a cheap recolour filter.

---

## 9.6 Rollback colour progression

Rollback should use colour narratively.

A recommended progression:

1. **Known-state colour**  
   Start with the current department and portal palette.

2. **Desaturation**  
   Strip active colours as the current simulation is frozen.

3. **Neutral continuity state**  
   Move into beige, CRT grey, black, pale cyan, and off-white.

4. **Memory extraction colours**  
   Introduce dusty rose, amber, mint, lavender, and chrome.

5. **Anomaly colours**  
   Use brief hot magenta, warning red, acid lime, or RGB separation.

6. **Reconstruction colours**  
   Reintroduce department colour as a system-calibration layer.

7. **Portal-ready colour**  
   Stabilise into the clean Foxglove interface palette.

---

## 9.7 Colour behaviour rules

### Rule 1

Most frames should have **one dominant tonal identity**, not ten competing colours.

### Rule 2

Colour should often emerge gradually rather than appearing all at once.

### Rule 3

Corruption colours should be rarer and more striking because they are restrained elsewhere.

### Rule 4

Foxglove should always remain slightly more elegant than chaotic.

# 11. Typography doctrine

## 10.1 Typographic philosophy

Typography in the sequence has **three roles**:

1. **System communication**
2. **Visual material**
3. **Atmospheric branding**

Typography should never feel accidental.  
It is one of the main storytelling devices.

---

## 10.2 Type families by function

### A. Terminal monospace

Used for:

- ASCII reconstruction
- system logs
- diagnostic strings
- data texture
- glitch material
- metadata particles
- continuity audit output
- retained-state manifests
- deletion and reconstruction fields

Characteristics:

- narrow
- bitmap-feeling
- early-terminal proportions
- tightly spaced
- utilitarian
- tactile
- low-level

---

### B. Retro GUI sans-serif

- retro enterprise software
- showroom UI
- old onboarding tools
- controlled desktop layers
- documentation interfaces
- product-demo screens
- continuity administration tools

Potential elements include:

- small dialog windows
- bordered system cards
- faux-desktop containers
- tab-like headers
- status bars
- progress indicators
- file or folder cards

- small checkboxes
- calendar-like boxes
- thin framed panels

- persistence summary cards
- continuity instance indicators

## 12.3 Liminal GUI rule

Windows and panels should often appear **disconnected from a full desktop ecosystem**.

Each deeper layer may become:

- more typographic
- more abstract
- more symmetrical

- procedural clarity
- retro GUI movement
- visual softness
- temporal restraint

### Accent

A few transitions may become more dramatic.

# 16. Scene system bible

Below is the recommended master scene grammar for startup and rollback.

---

## 15.1 Stage 1 — Cold boot

### Function

Establish that the system is starting.

### Mood

Quiet, technical, retro, understated.

### Visual behaviours

- black screen or very dark charcoal
- tiny system text
- low-intensity flicker
- no dramatic reveal yet
- maybe a small cursor or low-level line-by-line text appearance

### Example content

- FOXGLOVE MULTIVERSAL
- EMPLOYEE ENVIRONMENT
- INITIALISING
- LOADING CONTINUITY MODEL

### Design rule

This must feel like a boot process, not a title card.

---

## 15.2 Stage 2 — Memory space formation

### Function

Establish that a digital environment is being allocated.

### Mood

Liminal, soft, open, uncertain.

### Visual behaviours

- empty GUI-like space
- floating windows
- gradient field
- black void with system cards
- pale ASCII field emerging
- fragmented desktop logic

### Possible imagery

- a dialog box hovering in black
- a pale blue desktop-like fog
- a system panel in an empty space
- a large field of growing text

---

## 15.3 Stage 3 — Identity reconstruction

### Function

Suggest the player or employee is being built from data.

### Mood

Intimate, uncanny, elegant.

### Visual behaviours

- portrait reconstruction
- eye closeups
- profile silhouettes
- foxglove flower from data
- employee-ID-like imagery
- text-as-image logic

### Key doctrine

This is one of the most important stages.  
It must feel like **identity is not assumed; it is assembled**.

---

## 15.4 Stage 4 — Corporate optimism layer

### Function

Introduce the retro-commercial vaporwave softness.

### Mood

Pleasant, polished, nostalgic, aspirational.

### Visual behaviours

- pastel gradients
- chrome accents
- showroom-like fields
- Japanese-commercial composition
- clean UI cards
- faux training-environment graphics

### Important

This stage is where the sequence can feel most beautiful and slightly kitsch.

---

## 15.5 Stage 5 — Liminal drift

### Function

Create atmosphere and strangeness.

### Mood

Empty, suspended, contemplative, eerie.

### Visual behaviours

- office without people
- floating dialog boxes
- impossible desktop space
- hallways reconstructed from text
- waiting-room digital emptiness
- soft panning and slow holding

### Rule

Do not rush this stage.  
Foxglove needs time to feel empty.

---

## 15.6 Stage 6 — Institutional memory fragments

### Function

Suggest previous simulations, buried records, or unstable internal memory.

### Mood

Fascinating, melancholy, slightly wrong.

### Visual behaviours

- quick image fragments
- eye built from metrics
- document field collapsing
- corridor made from employee names
- profile fading in and out
- symbolic animal in a small corrupted panel
- repeated phrases forming hidden faces

---

## 15.7 Stage 7 — Controlled anomaly

### Function

Deliver the first unmistakable sign that the system is not fully normal.

### Mood

Sharp, restrained, unsettling.

### Visual behaviours

- impossible status text
- direct gaze
- hidden message
- continuity variance
- window layer error
- brief terminal contradiction

### Key doctrine

This moment should feel intentional, not noisy.  
It should be **brief enough to doubt, clear enough to register**.

---

## 15.8 Stage 8 — Correction or suppression

### Function

Reassert control.

### Mood

Corporate calm, over-managed normality.

### Visual behaviours

- overwrite
- reset
- message dismissal
- state normalisation
- display artifact resolved
- no user action required

This is one of the most Foxglove-feeling gestures possible.

---

## 15.9 Stage 9 — Convergence

### Function

Resolve the sequence into Foxglove identity.

### Mood

Controlled, ceremonial, stabilising.

### Visual behaviours

- particles converge
- windows collapse inward
- text grids align
- logo emerges from distortion
- palette stabilises
- glitch reduces
- brand mark becomes clean

---

## 15.10 Stage 10 — Portal handoff

### Function

Pass into the actual game.

### Mood

Professional, controlled, unexpectedly ordinary.

### Visual behaviours

- clean interface
- reduced texture
- less bloom
- straightforward system clarity
- calm brand presentation

### Example content

- EMPLOYEE ENVIRONMENT READY
- Foxglove Multiversal
- Tomorrow’s Work, Done Yesterday.

# 17. Rollback sequence master structure

The rollback sequence requires a larger scene grammar than ordinary startup.

---

## 16.1 Rollback Stage A — Authorisation

### Function

Confirm that the player has initiated rollback.

### Mood

Administrative, consequential, calm.

### Visuals

- a restrained confirmation window
- current portal still visible beneath
- system status shifting from active to locked
- department colours beginning to desaturate

### Example copy

- ROLLBACK REQUEST RECEIVED
- CURRENT SIMULATION WILL BE TERMINATED
- AUTHORISATION CONFIRMED
- CONTINUITY PROCESS STARTING

### Rule

Do not treat this like a dramatic explosion.  
The horror comes from how routine the system makes it feel.

---

## 16.2 Rollback Stage B — Environment suspension

### Function

Freeze the current run.

### Mood

Still, emptied, clinical.

### Visuals

- main UI stops moving
- timers halt
- panels lose colour
- values freeze
- live interface elements convert into static records
- the portal begins to recede into black or pastel emptiness

### Possible copy

- ACTIVE WORKSPACE SUSPENDED
- INPUT DISABLED
- FINAL STATE CAPTURE IN PROGRESS

---

## 16.3 Rollback Stage C — Final state capture

### Function

Record the completed run.

### Mood

Documentary, archival, elegiac.

### Visuals

- UI panels flatten into screenshots or records
- values become monospaced text
- job, training, document, department, and progression data flow into an archive field
- an employee portrait becomes a text-built profile record
- the current simulation is visually converted into data

### Important

This stage should feel like the current world is being remembered only as documentation.

---

## 16.4 Rollback Stage D — Persistence audit

### Function

Separate retained and non-retained state.

### Mood

Bureaucratic, uncanny, exact.

### Visuals

- data splits into columns, windows, or spatial regions
- persistent resources glow or remain legible
- disposable state fades, fragments, or is classified
- authorised memory is highlighted
- non-persistent data is released into black

### Possible copy

- PERSISTENCE AUDIT
- AUTHORISED RETENTION
- NON-PERSISTENT STATE
- RECOVERY VALUE
- CONTINUITY EXCEPTIONS
- ADMIN CODES
- ACHIEVEMENT RECORDS
- DISCOVERED SYNERGIES

### Narrative importance

This stage should make persistence feel like an institutional permission, not a magical reward.

---

## 16.5 Rollback Stage E — Recovery value calculation

### Function

Translate run depth and productivity into retained recurrence value.

### Mood

Evaluative, opaque, official.

### Visuals

- a numerical or abstract score assembles from run-state fragments
- department-standing, time, progression, and run depth may appear as partial inputs
- a clean final value resolves from noisy calculation fields
- avoid turning the scene into a conventional spreadsheet

### Possible copy

- RUN PRODUCTIVITY ASSESSMENT
- DEPTH FACTOR
- CONTINUITY CONTRIBUTION
- RECOVERY VALUE CALCULATED

### Rule

The visual should imply evaluation without exposing every formula unless the game intentionally wants that transparency.

---

## 16.6 Rollback Stage F — Active simulation collapse

### Function

Terminate the existing world.

### Mood

Beautiful, melancholy, irreversible.

### Visuals

- office scenes dissolve into text
- windows fold inward
- portrait fragments detach
- document fields erode into empty space
- the current department palette drains away
- UI geometry compresses toward a centre or horizon
- liminal office spaces remain briefly after their data has disappeared

### Key doctrine

The world should not explode.  
It should be **administratively removed**.

---

## 16.7 Rollback Stage G — Institutional memory passage

### Function

Expose deeper fragments while the system is between simulations.

### Mood

Dreamlike, nostalgic, unsettling.

### Visuals

This is the ideal place for the complete retro-vaporwave vocabulary:

- Macintosh Plus-like monochrome simplicity
- Windows 95-like floating windows
- Japanese-commercial product-stage layouts
- pastel horizon fields
- chrome system cards
- empty office corridors
- employee portraits made from ASCII
- hidden faces inside repeated corporate phrases
- small corrupted media panels
- symbolic eyes
- foxglove forms
- recursion chambers
- system shrines
- beige-computer materiality
- floating icons in a black or pastel void

### Canon rule

This is the **visual heyday within the rollback heyday**: the most expressive section of the entire sequence.

---

## 16.8 Rollback Stage H — Controlled anomaly escalation

### Function

Allow rollback to expose more than ordinary startup.

### Mood

Uncertain, direct, restrained but unmistakable.

### Possible anomaly content

- YOU HAVE BEEN HERE BEFORE
- EMPLOYEE INSTANCE MISMATCH
- PREVIOUS USER STATE PRESENT
- UNAUTHORISED RECALL DETECTED
- MEMORY RETENTION EXCEEDS POLICY
- STATUS: OBSERVING
- CONTINUITY SOURCE UNKNOWN

### Behaviour

- anomaly appears
- system pauses or stutters
- colour channels misalign
- a face or eye may resolve too clearly
- the system overwrites the message
- a calm recovery notice follows

### Rule

The anomaly should not derail rollback.  
Foxglove absorbs it and continues.

---

## 16.9 Rollback Stage I — Core reboot

### Function

Restart the full system.

### Mood

Architectural, ceremonial, abstract.

### Visuals

- typography tunnels
- recursive chambers
- symmetrical ASCII architecture
- geometric convergence
- status layers passing in sequence
- fields labelled PERSONNEL, DEPARTMENT, TENURE, PRODUCTIVITY, CONTINUITY
- a deeper hidden layer may flash too quickly to confirm
- the screen may briefly reach near-total black

### Possible copy

- CORE SERVICES OFFLINE
- CONTINUITY KERNEL RESTARTING
- EMPLOYEE MODEL CLEARED
- NEW INSTANCE PREPARING

---

## 16.10 Rollback Stage J — Identity reseeding

### Function

Rebuild the employee for the next run.

### Mood

Intimate, calm, uncanny.

### Visuals

- an employee silhouette or portrait reforms
- fields return in layers
- department colour is reintroduced
- persistent elements appear as stable anchors
- non-persistent details remain absent or blank
- the player may be returned to profile creation or a partially blank identity state

### Possible copy

- EMPLOYEE MODEL REBUILDING
- PROFILE SEED READY
- DEPARTMENT ALIGNMENT PENDING
- CONTINUITY MARKERS APPLIED

---

## 16.11 Rollback Stage K — Foxglove convergence

### Function

Reassert institutional identity after world reconstruction.

### Mood

Formal, beautiful, controlled.

### Visuals

- thousands of glyphs converge
- retro GUI fragments align
- foxglove flower emerges
- logo initially contains gaps, colour offsets, or text spill
- mark stabilises into official branding

### Rule

The clean logo should feel like the system successfully concealing the complexity beneath it.

---

## 16.12 Rollback Stage L — New portal handoff

### Function

Return the player to the newly initialised simulation.

### Mood

Professional, calm, slightly too normal.

### Visuals

- distortion disappears
- clean portal UI appears
- the new run begins
- retained elements are visible
- reset elements are absent or blank
- system availability returns to a controlled state

### Example copy

- NEW CONTINUITY INSTANCE READY
- EMPLOYEE ENVIRONMENT AVAILABLE
- WELCOME BACK

The phrase “WELCOME BACK” may be used cautiously because it can become narratively loaded.

# 23. Implementation doctrine

## 22.1 High-level implementation principle

The sequence should feel richly layered, but the implementation system should remain conceptually clean.

Build the sequence from reusable layer types:

- background field
- GUI layer
- text-art layer
- blur or glow layer
- scanline or noise layer
- accent motion layer
- anomaly layer
- state-audit layer
- rollback-transition layer
- logo-convergence layer

---

## 22.2 Layer stack recommendation

A typical shot may consist of:

1. **Base field**  
   black, gradient, or pastel void

2. **Primary compositional object**  
   window, portrait, flower, corridor, card, office fragment, or continuity structure

3. **ASCII reconstruction layer**  
   text-generated structure

4. **Glow or bloom copy**  
   blurred duplicate

5. **CRT treatment**  
   scanlines, flicker, minor softness

6. **GUI overlay fragments**  
   small labels, boxes, state text

7. **Instability layer**  
   RGB split, detached glyphs, row tearing

8. **Mask or erosion layer**  
   dissolving edges, field interruptions

9. **State classification layer**  
   retained, archived, released, quarantined, rebuilt

10. **Final grade or vignette**  
    unify the frame

---

## 22.3 Figma and design-system mindset

Even though the final sequence is animated, design should first think in terms of **systemised shot families**.

Create reusable design modules for:

- boot text block
- retro dialog box
- floating panel
- foxglove formation shot
- identity reconstruction shot
- liminal office shot
- anomaly shot
- clean handoff shot
- rollback authorisation shot
- persistence audit shot
- system collapse shot
- core reboot chamber
- identity reseeding shot
- continuity-ready handoff

This will keep the sequence coherent.

---

## 22.4 Reusability doctrine

The same assets may recur, but their meaning should evolve.

For example:

- the same employee portrait can appear stable at startup, dissolve during rollback, and reform in the next run
- the same Foxglove flower can appear as a small boot emblem, a large rollback convergence, and a late-game corrupted symbol
- the same dialog box can begin as mundane and later display continuity anomalies
- the same office corridor can become progressively less trustworthy across recurrences

This supports narrative continuity while controlling production cost.

---

## 22.5 Performance and fallback doctrine

The sequence should be designed with graceful degradation.

Possible levels:

### Full presentation

- complete animation
- ASCII reconstruction
- CRT effects
- layered transitions
- rollback memory passage
- logo convergence

### Reduced-effects mode

- lower particle count
- fewer blur passes
- simplified RGB separation
- fewer live text transformations
- same narrative beats preserved

### Minimal or accessibility mode

- static or lightly animated cards
- clean system messages
- reduced flicker
- no rapid glitch
- preserved rollback-stage meaning
- optional skip after first complete viewing

The sequence must not become inaccessible merely to preserve style.

# 24. Lifecycle and heyday doctrine

## 23.1 First startup

The first startup should introduce:

- retro corporate OS language
- small ASCII reconstruction
- a hint of liminal space
- gentle CRT softness
- a brief Foxglove formation
- no major lore reveal

It should feel distinctive but not exhaustive.

---

## 23.2 Routine startup

Routine startup should be shorter and more stable.

It may:

- use a compact version
- show one or two rotating motifs
- adapt to player department
- reflect current stability or lucidity
- occasionally expose tiny anomalies

It should not replay the full rollback sequence every time the player launches the game.

---

## 23.3 First rollback

The first rollback is the moment the visual style enters its prime.

It should:

- be substantially longer than routine startup
- introduce the full system reboot structure
- show state capture and persistence audit
- expose institutional memory fragments
- deliver the strongest liminal vaporwave section so far
- establish that rollback affects the whole system
- return the player to a newly initialised environment

This is the sequence’s first major artistic and narrative payoff.

---

## 23.4 Later rollbacks

Later rollbacks should evolve rather than simply repeat.

Possible changes:

- shorter familiar stages
- new memory fragments
- more explicit anomalies
- different retained-state visualisations
- deeper system layers
- stronger recurrence recognition
- altered Foxglove logo behaviour
- changes based on lucidity, stability, grade, or run history
- fragments from prior runs
- increasingly suspicious system copy

---

## 23.5 Late-game rollback

Late-game rollback may reinterpret the entire doctrine.

It may:

- break the clean handoff
- preserve an anomaly into the portal
- reveal a continuity layer previously hidden
- show that employee identity is not fully reset
- display memories the system cannot suppress
- invert the normal colour progression
- allow the player to recognise earlier startup imagery as literal system operations

The style’s lifecycle should therefore move from aesthetic intrigue to narrative evidence.

# 25. Quality bar and review criteria

Every startup and rollback shot should be reviewed against the following questions.

## 24.1 Does it feel Foxglove?

If the logo were removed, would it still feel like the world of Foxglove?

## 24.2 Does it balance beauty and wrongness?

If it is only pretty, it is too shallow.  
If it is only creepy, it has lost the retro-soft seduction.

## 24.3 Does it feel like a boot or reboot process?

Or does it feel like a random montage?

## 24.4 Is the retro language coherent?

Or is it just visual name-dropping?

## 24.5 Is the liminality present?

Does the frame feel suspended, waiting, or partially unoccupied?

## 24.6 Is the corruption restrained?

Is there enough control that the anomaly moments still matter?

## 24.7 Is the copywriting correct?

Does the wording feel internal, procedural, and slightly bureaucratic?

## 24.8 Does it hand off cleanly?

Does the sequence resolve into a believable portal entry?

## 24.9 Does rollback feel larger than startup?

If the two are visually equivalent, the lifecycle has failed.

## 24.10 Does rollback communicate whole-system reinitialisation?

The player should feel that the entire Foxglove environment has been taken offline and rebuilt.

## 24.11 Is persistence visually meaningful?

Retained elements should feel deliberately extracted and reapplied, not simply left untouched.

## 24.12 Does the sequence work without audio?

The animation must communicate state and emotion visually.

# 29. Implementation-facing canonical prompt

Design and implement the Foxglove rollback sequence as a **complete system reboot in which the machine reconstructs reality through text**, not as a decorative reset transition.

The animation should use the established **liminal corporate vaporwave** doctrine:

- Macintosh Plus-era computing restraint
- Windows 95 and Windows 98 workplace GUI culture
- Japanese commercial graphic composition
- tasteful 1980s and 1990s kitsch futurism
- CRT bloom and scanlines
- pastel and chrome gradients
- empty liminal digital space
- ASCII and ANSI text-image reconstruction
- restrained corruption
- procedural corporate copy
- uncanny institutional memory

Ordinary game startup should use a concise and stable subset of this language.

The sequence’s full heyday belongs to rollback.

When rollback begins:

1. Freeze and suspend the active employee environment.
2. Capture the final state of the current simulation.
3. Convert the current rendered world into its archival and textual substrate.
4. Audit the run for persistent and non-persistent state.
5. Calculate Recovery Value and other approved carry-forward values.
6. Visually separate authorised persistence from discarded state.
7. Administratively dismantle the current simulation.
8. Pass through a longer liminal memory-reconstruction sequence.
9. Expose one or more brief continuity anomalies.
10. Suppress or classify those anomalies without halting the process.
11. Restart the Foxglove continuity core.
12. Rebuild the employee model and visible world from text, metadata, and retained records.
13. Reintroduce department identity and retained state.
14. Reform the Foxglove mark from unstable ASCII and GUI fragments.
15. Hand the player into a clean, newly initialised portal.

The player should feel that the current world has ended and the entire Foxglove system has been rebooted, while the interface itself remains calm, polite, retro, beautiful, and bureaucratically controlled.

Do not rely on music or audio-reactive timing.

Do not present rollback as a generic prestige reward.

Do not use constant glitch effects.

Do not exhaust the full visual language during routine startup.

The completed rollback should feel like a controlled corporate rebirth: a ceremonial systems procedure in which reality is reduced to records, authorised memory survives, and the employee is reconstructed for another simulation.
